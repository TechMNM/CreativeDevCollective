Children officially become learners, in the language of schooling. Grade R learners move from mostly unstructured learning to structured learning, where they learn to sit quietly and have structured lessons with a formal curriculum for the first time. One can expect your child to:

  • • understand rhyming and play rhyming games
  • • match some spoken and written words
  • • understand that print is read from left to right, top to bottom
  • • write some letters and numbers
  • • recognise some familiar words
  • • predict what will happen next in a story
  • • retell stories that have been read to them
